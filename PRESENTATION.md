# Production Process Page — Section List (for the presentation)

**One page, two audiences:** the *curious visitor* (brand value) and the *waiting customer*
(reassurance). Every section serves this dual goal.

**Narrative spine:** *Reassure first, impress second.*
Hero & Reframe set the frame → Film & Journey prove it → Technik & People build credibility →
FAQ removes doubt → Finale drives action.

---

| # | Section | Content | Why we need it | Audience |
|---|---------|---------|----------------|----------|
| 1 | **Hero — "In Reutlingen wird es Ihr Stück"** | "Designed in Germany" eyebrow, large headline, primary CTA *Den Weg verfolgen*, subtle order-status link, trust facts (4.72★, name engraving), workshop image | First 5 seconds: establish premium + "Designed in Germany", pull the visitor into the story — without implying that delays are routine | Both |
| 2 | **Reframe — "We don't make to stock. We make for you."** | One bold statement + short paragraph + subtle "already ordered?" link | Reframes the production time as an *advantage* (made-to-order, longevity) rather than a delay | Both |

## Content guardrails (briefing-compliant)

- **"Designed in Germany"** — never "Made in Germany".
- **No blanket "stainless steel"** (mailbox body = powder-coated steel; plates = stainless/V2A).
- **No concrete delivery times** — tone is "very fast"; the tracker shows *where*, not *when*.
- **Warranty** only ever shown with a link to the warranty terms.
- Provable figures: avg. 4.72★ · 33,500+ reviews · since 2013, 1.8M+ homeowners · 165 employees.
